Information and Consent


It’s not what you say, it’s how you say it: Creating the Prosodic Proficiency Test Battery.

You are invited to participate in a research study of Radboud University, the Netherlands. Your participation in the study is voluntary and anonymous.

Outline and aim of the research study

We want to evaluate the efficacy of a new online proficiency test that can be used to evaluate how well someone produces the rhythm, intonation and word stress of English. By comparing manual annotations of these so-called prosodic features to automatic annotations, we can assess the validity of our test, which can be used by (foreign) language learners, teachers, and researchers.

Who is conducting the research study?

This research is conducted by Ronny Bujok (Postdoc), in collaboration with Lieke van Maastricht (Principal Investigator).

What does participation in the study entail?

In this study you will fill out an online survey. You will answer general demographic questions and questions that test your knowledge of English prosody. For some of those questions, you will be prompted to record your own speech. Finally, you will complete a brief test of your musical abilities, where you are asked to indicate whether two sounds are the same or different. Filling out the survey will take approximately 30 minutes.

Voluntary participation

Your participation in this research is voluntary. This means that you can stop your participation and withdraw your consent at any time during the study, without giving a reason.

What data do we collect and how do we handle it?

In this study we collect research data in the form of a questionnaire and audio recordings using the microphone of your device (PC, laptop). These will be used for scientific purposes. The audio data will be coded by transforming your speech to numbers (pitch, intensity, and duration values) that cannot be traced back to your voice or you as a person. Your research data may be of interest to other scientific research. Your research data may be of interest to other scientific research. Therefore, if you consent to participate in this study below, your audio recordings may be shared with other researchers. The anonymous research data will be available to other scientists for at least 10 years.


This study makes use of the PROMS tool, which measures musical abilities. It is provided by researchers from University of Innsbruck and uses Limesurvey for data collection. Please note that Limesurvey collects system data, among which IP address, for error-free and optimized provision of the services. However, these data will not be processed by the research team and will be deleted from the system after data collection has ended. For more information read the information provided by University of Innsbruck about the data that limesurvey processes. Finally, the results from the PROMS tool are shared with the researchers who designed the test. These data are fully anonymized before they are shared and are only used to further develop the test.


To carry out the study, it is necessary that we collect, use and store personal data. These personal data are your age in years, native language, gender, education level, secondary languages, general proficiency level of English, and any visual-auditory and learning impairments that you may have. These data will be anonymous because there is no way to trace them back exclusively to you.


All anonymous personal data will be shared with researchers outside the research team in a data repository. Personal data necessary to answer the research question will be kept for at least 10 years. All research and anonymous personal data are necessary to answer the research question are safely stored following the Radboud University guidelines.


Access for control purposes

Some persons need to have access to your personal and research data. This is necessary to check whether the data have been stored in accordance with the rules. Persons who carry out this check are, for example, the data officer and designated data management staff at Radboud University. They will keep your data confidential.


Your consent

Your data may only be used for our study if you give permission for this. Because the data is immediately anonymous, it is not possible to have your research data removed after the completion of the experiment.


Do you have any questions?

If you have any questions or would like more information about the research study, please contact Lieke van Maastricht (lieke.vanmaastricht@ru.nl). For other questions about this study (e.g., data processing), please contact privacy@let.ru.nl


Ethical assessment and complaints

This research study has been approved by the Ethics Assessment Committee Humanities of Radboud University (EACH file number 2025-2438).


Should you have any complaints regarding this research, please contact the researcher.

You can also file a complaint with the secretary of the Ethics Assessment Committee Humanities of Radboud University (etc-gw@ru.nl).
